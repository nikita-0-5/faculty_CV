Research

Research Focus

  • Role of Songs in Prasad's Dramatic Structure — Doctoral and ongoing research exploring how Natya Geet (dramatic songs) function as structural and emotive devices in the plays of Jaishankar Prasad, one of Hindi literature's foremost dramatists.
  • Natak and Rangmanch — Scholarly investigation into the relationship between the written dramatic text and its theatrical realization on stage, with emphasis on Hindi theatre traditions.
  • Kavya Shashtra (Hindi Poetics) — Research and teaching in the classical theory of Hindi poetics, including aesthetic analysis of lyrical forms embedded in dramatic works.
  • Comparative Literary Studies — Analysis of Hindi prose and narrative forms, including short fiction (Kahani) and essays (Nibandh), situating them within broader literary traditions.

Research Guidance

  • Actively engaged in mentoring research scholars in Hindi literature with a focus on drama, theatre studies, and literary criticism at the University of Delhi.
  • Guides students through the interdisciplinary intersection of performance studies, literary theory, and Hindi poetics (Kavya Shashtra).
